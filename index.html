Cores & Threads â€“ What really counts?

The CPU runs the show. Physical Cores do the heavy lifting, while Threads (virtual cores) ensure there's no waiting time. Think of supermarket checkouts: Cores are the registers, threads are the cashiers scanning the next customer while the first is packing.

Gaming: 6-8 cores are plenty. More rarely adds FPS in games.
Productivity: Anyone editing or streaming welcomes every extra core (12+).

Architecture Check

Intel (P- & E-Cores): A mix of powerhouses for gaming and efficient cores for background tasks. Needs Windows 11 to run smoothly.
AMD (X3D): The "3D V-Cache" models have a massive cache directly on the chip. Often the top choice for gamers right now as it minimizes stuttering.

Clock speed isn't everything

Don't be blinded by GHz. A modern 4 GHz CPU runs circles around an old 5 GHz chip. The underlying technology (IPC) makes the difference.

VRAM â€“ How much memory do you need?

Video memory is like the trunk for textures. If it's full, things stutter.

  • 8 GB Enough for Full HD (1080p), but getting tight.
  • 12 GB The sweet spot for WQHD (1440p).
  • 16 GB+ Mandatory for 4K enjoyment.

ðŸš€ DLSS & FSR (Upscaling)

The magic trick: The card renders the image smaller (saving power) and an AI blows it up sharp. Looks almost as good as native, but brings significantly more FPS.

ðŸ’¡ Raytracing

Real light rays instead of fake shadows. Looks fantastic (reflections!), but eats performance for breakfast. NVIDIA is usually still ahead here.

RAM (Short-term memory)
  • Always Duo: Always buy a kit of 2 sticks ("Dual Channel"). Single sticks waste performance.
  • DDR5: The new standard. Fast, but watch the latency (CL30 is top).
  • Important: Turn on "XMP" or "EXPO" in BIOS after building, otherwise RAM runs slow!
Motherboard (Nervous System)
  • B-Series (e.g. B650): The rational choice. Has everything gamers need.
  • Z/X-Series: Only for people who want to overclock or need 10 SSDs.
  • VRM Cooling: Make sure there are metal heatsinks around the CPU socket. Bare boards get hot.

Forget old hard drives (HDDs) for games. They just slow you down. M.2 NVMe is the standard.

Tech Speed Verdict
SATA SSD ~500 MB/s Okay for mass storage, but outdated.
NVMe PCIe 4.0 ~7.000 MB/s The Sweetspot. Buy!
NVMe PCIe 5.0 ~12.000+ MB/s Expensive, hot, and barely noticeably faster in daily use.

IPS vs. OLED

IPS is the standard: Good colors, fair prices.
OLED is luxury: Perfect black, insanely fast, but more expensive and requires some care (burn-in).

Resolution & Hz

Below 144 Hz is a no-go for gaming today.

Mechanical Keyboards

The feel decides. Red switches are linear and fast (no click). Blue are noisy (clicky). Brown are a good middle ground.

Mice

DPI numbers of 25,000 are marketing nonsense. More important: A good shape and low weight (under 70g is trendy for shooters). Wireless is just as fast as wired nowadays.

Those who save here pay twice. A bad power supply can grill your whole PC.

First Aid Kit

Don't panic. Most PC building problems are classics and easy to solve.

  1. Debug LEDs: Look at the motherboard (usually top right). Is a small light on (CPU, DRAM, VGA)? That reveals the culprit.
  2. Monitor Cable: Classic mistake. The cable must go into the graphics card at the bottom, not the motherboard at the top!
  3. Power: Are the thick 24-pin connector and the 8-pin CPU connector (top left) really tight?
  4. RAM: Memory often needs to be pushed in with decent force until it clicks loudly.

No worries, nothing broken. DDR5 always runs in safety mode out of the box.

Solution: Go into BIOS (hammer 'Del' or 'F2' at start) and activate the profile XMP (Intel) or EXPO (AMD). Save & Restart (F10) â€“ done.

DVDs are dead. You need a USB stick (8GB+).
Google "Windows Media Creation Tool" on another PC. The program makes your stick bootable.
Stick in the new PC -> Turn on -> Boot from stick -> Install.

Sounds like an electrical buzz? That's "Coil Whine". Physically caused and unfortunately not a defect (exchange is goodwill).
Trick: Limit FPS in games to your monitor Hz (e.g. 144). Less load = less whine.

Short answer: Take Windows 11.
Especially if you have a newer Intel CPU, you need Win 11 so the PC knows which cores to use for games ("Thread Director"). HDR also looks much better in 11.
